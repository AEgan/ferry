About ferry

Ferry is a command-line tool rubygem designed for data migrations and manipulation, maintained as an open-source project by the students of Carnegie Mellon's Information Systems department (currently: Anthony Corletti and Logan Watanabe). The inspiration for ferry was kindled from collective internship experiences and from the growing prevalence of big data migration and manipulation challenges that companies, corporations, universities, and organizations face in today's information age.

It's important to note that not only are technology companies facing these big data issues, but that with the rise of the information age, more and more data is circulated around us, bringing these challenges to organizations that do not employ their own technical staff to manage these problems. The objective of ferry is to streamline the process of dealing with these big data problems, by making your data work for you, by entering a simple command from your terminal.

What can I use ferry for?

  • Data Migration & Manipulation
    • Exporting and importing data to and from various file formats (.csv, .yml, .sql, etc.)
    • Migrating data to and from various 3rd party storage services (Amazon S3, Google Cloud Storage, Rackspace, MS Azure, etc.)
    • Database switcher: Being able to quickly switch from one database type to another.

Documentation & Support

The easiest way to get started with ferry is to fork the code at our GitHub repo and start hacking.

Examples
Exporting
  • $ ferry --to_csv environment_name,table_name
  • $ ferry --to_yaml environment_name,table_name
Importing
  • $ ferry --import environment_name,table_name,file_name_to_be_imported
Database Switching
  • $ ferry --db_switch environment_name,desired_adapter
Personalized Migrations
  • $ ferry --init
